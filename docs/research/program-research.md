# Summer 2026 Pre-College STEM Programs
## Comprehensive Research Summary

*Compiled December 2025*

This document provides comprehensive information on 21 competitive summer programs for high school students, including application deadlines, eligibility requirements, costs, and insider knowledge for the Summer 2026 application cycle.

# 1. Research Science Institute (RSI)
**Website:** cee.org

## Dates
- **Application Opens:** October 2025
- **Application Deadline:** December 10, 2025 at 11:59 PM ET
- **Decisions:** March 2026
- **Program Dates:** June 28 – August 8, 2026 (6 weeks)

## Eligibility
- **Grade:** Current juniors (rising seniors)
- **Age:** Must be 16+ by July 1, 2026
- **Citizenship:** U.S. citizens/permanent residents apply directly; international students through country-specific processes
- **Recommended Scores:** PSAT Math ≥740, EBRW ≥700; ACT Math ≥33, Verbal ≥34

## Application Requirements
- Online application with essays about STEM goals
- 2-3 teacher recommendations (math/science or research supervisor)
- Official transcript
- Standardized test scores (PSAT, SAT, ACT, AP)
- **Application Fee:** $65-75 (waivers available)

## Program Details
- **Format:** Residential at MIT campus, Cambridge, MA
- **Size:** 100 students (2/3 U.S., 1/3 international)
- **Cost:** FREE (covers tuition, room, board)

## Program Structure
Week 1: Intensive STEM classes with professors. Weeks 2-6: Individual research projects with mentors at MIT/Boston-area labs. Week 6: Written papers and oral presentations.

## Rich Context
- **Acceptance Rate:** ~2-3% (100 accepted from ~3,000 applicants)
- **Prestige Level:** Most prestigious pre-college STEM program. Selectivity comparable to Harvard/Yale/Princeton.
- **Outcomes:** Alumni have 90% MIT acceptance rate, most attend Ivy+. Students publish research, compete in Intel STS, Siemens Competition, ISEF.
- **Insider Tip:** About 10% have no prior research experience. Selection based on superior academic achievement, leadership potential, math/science competitions, university coursework, or original research. AI use in applications strictly prohibited.

# 2. Summer Science Program (SSP)
**Website:** summerscience.org

## Dates
- **Application Opens:** Late December 2025
- **International Deadline:** January 22, 2026
- **Domestic Deadline:** February 19, 2026
- **Decisions:** International mid-March, Domestic mid-April 2026
- **Program Dates:** Multiple 5-week sessions (late June – early August)

## Eligibility
- **Grade:** Current juniors (rising seniors); sophomores occasionally accepted
- **Age:** 15-18 during program
- **Prerequisites by Track:** 
  - Astrophysics: physics + precalculus
  - Biochemistry: biology + chemistry + strong algebra
  - Genomics: biology + Algebra 2
  - Synthetic Chemistry: chemistry courses

## Application Requirements
- **Application Fee:** FREE
- Online form with personal statement
- 2 teacher recommendations (STEM preferred)
- Unofficial transcript
- NO standardized test scores required (except English proficiency for international students)

## Program Details
- **Format:** Residential at 10+ campuses (UC Boulder, UNC Chapel Hill, New Mexico State, Purdue, Indiana University, others)
- **Size:** ~600-700 students total (36 per campus)
- **Cost:** $9,800 (2025 rate). FREE for families earning <$75K; discounts for <$140K; $3,000 stipends available for students who need summer earnings

## Research Tracks
- **Astrophysics:** Asteroid orbit determination
- **Biochemistry:** Fungal enzyme inhibition
- **Bacterial Genomics:** Antibiotic resistance
- **Synthetic Chemistry:** Macrocyclic catalysts
- **Cell Biology:** CRISPR gene editing

## Program Structure
Teams of 3 students complete full research cycle from data collection to analysis. 60+ hours of research work. Field trips, guest speakers, social activities.

## Rich Context
- **Acceptance Rate:** 10-15%
- **History:** Founded 1959, one of oldest residential STEM programs. 2022 received $200M bequest from Qualcomm co-founder Franklin Antonio.
- **Admissions:** Need-blind, holistic review considering motivation, resources, obstacles overcome. Encourages underrepresented students and first-gen college.
- **Outcomes:** 85% attend top-30 universities, 60% at top-10. Strong alumni network (2,500+ alumni). Optional College Link mentorship program.

- **Decisions:** Mid-April 2026
- **Program Dates:** 6 weeks (mid-June – late July 2026)

## Application Requirements

- School transcripts
- Teacher recommendations
- Essays about interests/goals

- Participants typically 1/3 female

- Yellow Pig Day (July 17) tradition
- Alumni include MacArthur Fellows (Eric Lander, Erik Winfree), Lisa Randall, Dana Randall
- Strong alumni community with annual reunions
- Emphasis on mathematical discovery and community
- Can only attend once

- **Application Opens:** December 15, 2025
- **Application Deadline:** March 9, 2026
- **Decisions:** Rolling, approximately 3-6 weeks after application completion

- **Grade:** Current 10th or 11th graders (outstanding 9th graders case-by-case)

- **Commitment:** Must attend entire program, cannot enroll in other courses/activities/programs during RMP
- **International Students:** TOEFL/English proficiency NOT required

## Application Requirements
- Online application
- Transcript (must include Fall 2025 grades – first trimester or semester)
- AP Scores (optional, if taken)
- Personal Statement (500 words max – propose a process-based research question)
- Short Responses (4 questions, 150 words each)
- NO recommendations required for 2026
- **Application Fee:** $75 (non-refundable)

# 21. UCSB Summer Research Academies (SRA)
**Website:** summer.ucsb.edu/programs/summer-research-academies

## Dates
- **Application Opens:** December 15, 2025
- **Application Deadline:** March 23, 2026
- **Decisions:** Rolling, approximately 3-5 weeks after application completion
- **Program Dates:** June 26 – July 24, 2026 (4 weeks)

## Eligibility
- **Grade:** Current 9th, 10th, or 11th graders
- **GPA:** Minimum 3.60 academic weighted GPA
- **Commitment:** Must attend entire program, cannot enroll in other courses/activities/programs during SRA
- **International Students:** TOEFL/English proficiency NOT required

## Application Requirements
- Online application
- Transcript (must include Fall 2025 grades)
- Personal Statement (500 words max – why chosen track interests you, what questions excited to explore)
- Short Responses (4 questions, 150 words each)
- **Application Fee:** $75 (non-refundable)

## Program Details
- **Format:** Residential or Commuter at UC Santa Barbara
- **Cost:**
  - Commuter: $3,475 total ($75 app fee + $900 deposit + $2,500 tuition/fees)
  - Residential: $9,874 total (adds $6,399 housing/meals)
- **Financial Aid:** Limited need-based scholarships, priority to California residents

## 2026 Research Tracks (12 options)
1. **Probabilistic Computing** – Machine learning, energy-efficient AI
2. **Homo Technologicus** – Philosophy of technology
3. **Invisible Cities** – Urban data analysis, bias in data
4. **The Gene Edit** – CRISPR, gene editing for disorders
5. **Photon Forge** – Photonics, integrated optical circuits
6. **Digital Frontlines** – Online social movements
7. **Molecular Vision** – Structural biology, cryoEM
8. **Modeling Impact** – Policy analysis, demographics
9. **Moral Medicine** – Bioethics
10. **Rethinking AI** – History and philosophy of AI
11. **Expression Intelligence** – Facial expression analysis
12. **Robotics Revealed** – Robotics dynamics and control

## Program Structure
Welcome Event, Academic Intensive, then 4 weeks on campus. Form research groups (teams of 3), develop research question, hands-on labs, lectures, discussions. 25-40 hours/week research. GRIT talks lecture series. Earn 4 university credits. Final capstone seminar presentation.

## Rich Context
- Selective program (exact rate not published)
- More accessible than RMP (lower GPA requirement, open to 9th graders)
- Group-based research (vs. individual in RMP)
- Students become registered UCSB students with access to campus facilities
- Research can be continued remotely with instructor consent
- Good stepping stone before applying to more competitive programs
